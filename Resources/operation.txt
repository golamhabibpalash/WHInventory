Warehouse & Inventory Management System - Operation Guide

Purpose
This guide is for training a team to operate the application module by module. Each section explains the main purpose of the module and the common user operations.


1. Login and User Access

Purpose:
Users must log in before using the system. Access to pages depends on assigned roles.

Operations:
- Open the application URL.
- Enter registered email and password.
- After login, confirm that the dashboard and side navigation are visible.
- Use the profile menu to view or update personal information.
- Use Log Out after finishing work.

Training Notes:
- Train users not to share passwords.
- Make sure each user has only the roles needed for their job.


2. Dashboard

Purpose:
The dashboard gives a quick overview of business and inventory activity.

Operations:
- Open Dashboard > Default.
- Review summary cards, charts, and recent activity.
- Use dashboard information only as a quick status view.
- For detailed checking, open the related report module.


3. Company Setup

Purpose:
Company setup stores business information used in documents, reports, and footer details.

Operations:
- Open Settings > My Company.
- Review company name, address, website, currency, and other details.
- Update company information when required.
- Save changes and refresh the page if the footer or topbar data does not update immediately.


4. Tax Setup

Purpose:
Tax setup defines tax records used in purchase and sales transactions.

Operations:
- Open Settings > Tax.
- Create a new tax record with name/rate.
- Edit existing tax details if needed.
- Delete only unused or incorrect tax records.


5. Number Sequence

Purpose:
Number sequences generate document numbers for products, orders, stock movement, and other records.

Operations:
- Open Settings > Number Sequence.
- Review existing number formats.
- Update prefixes or sequence settings if business numbering changes.
- Avoid changing sequences during active operations unless approved.


6. Sales Master Data

Purpose:
Sales master data stores customer-related setup.

Modules:
- Customer Group
- Customer Category
- Customer
- Customer Contact

Operations:
- Create customer groups and categories first.
- Add customer records with correct contact, address, and business details.
- Add customer contacts under the relevant customer.
- Update customer information when business details change.
- Avoid duplicate customer records.


7. Purchase Master Data

Purpose:
Purchase master data stores vendor-related setup.

Modules:
- Vendor Group
- Vendor Category
- Vendor
- Vendor Contact

Operations:
- Create vendor groups and categories first.
- Add vendor records with correct business information.
- Add vendor contacts under the relevant vendor.
- Keep vendor records updated before creating purchase orders.
- Avoid duplicate vendor records.


8. Inventory Master Data

Purpose:
Inventory master data controls products, units, product groups, and warehouses.

Modules:
- Unit Measure
- Product Group
- Product
- Warehouse

Operations:
- Create unit measures such as pcs, kg, box, liter, etc.
- Create product groups to classify items.
- Create products with name, price, unit measure, product group, and physical/non-physical status.
- Create warehouses before stock transactions are entered.
- Keep product and warehouse records clean because they are used throughout the system.


9. Sales Order

Purpose:
Sales orders record customer orders before delivery.

Operations:
- Open Sales > Sales Order.
- Create a sales order for the selected customer.
- Add order items with product, quantity, price, tax, and other required fields.
- Save the order.
- Review the order before further processing.
- Generate or view PDF if needed.

Training Notes:
- Confirm customer and product details before saving.
- Do not create duplicate orders for the same customer request.


10. Delivery Order

Purpose:
Delivery orders record goods issued to customers and affect inventory movement.

Operations:
- Open Inventory > Delivery Order.
- Create a delivery order based on the customer/sales transaction.
- Select warehouse and products.
- Confirm quantities before saving.
- Save the delivery order.
- Print or view delivery PDF if required.

Training Notes:
- Delivery should be entered only after physical goods are ready to dispatch.
- Check stock availability before delivery.


11. Sales Return

Purpose:
Sales returns record goods returned by customers.

Operations:
- Open Inventory > Sales Return.
- Create a return record for the related customer/delivery.
- Select returned products and quantities.
- Confirm the warehouse receiving the returned stock.
- Save the return.
- Review stock impact after saving.


12. Purchase Order

Purpose:
Purchase orders record items ordered from vendors.

Operations:
- Open Purchase > Purchase Order.
- Select vendor.
- Add products, quantities, price, tax, and other details.
- Save the order.
- Review the order before sending to vendor.
- Generate or view PDF if needed.

Training Notes:
- Confirm vendor and item pricing before saving.
- Keep purchase order records aligned with vendor documents.


13. Goods Receive

Purpose:
Goods receive records stock received from vendors and increases warehouse stock.

Operations:
- Open Inventory > Goods Receive.
- Create a goods receive record.
- Select vendor/purchase reference where applicable.
- Select warehouse, products, and received quantities.
- Save the record.
- Check inventory reports after receiving.

Training Notes:
- Enter goods receive only after physical stock is received.
- Match received quantity with supplier invoice or challan.


14. Purchase Return

Purpose:
Purchase returns record goods returned to vendors.

Operations:
- Open Inventory > Purchase Return.
- Create a return record for the selected vendor.
- Select products, warehouse, and return quantities.
- Save the return.
- Review stock movement after saving.


15. Stock Transfer

Purpose:
Stock transfer moves inventory between warehouses.

Modules:
- Transfer Out
- Transfer In

Operations:
- Open Inventory > Transfer Out.
- Create transfer out from the source warehouse.
- Select products and quantities.
- Save the transfer out record.
- Open Inventory > Transfer In.
- Receive the transferred stock into the destination warehouse.
- Confirm quantities and save.

Training Notes:
- Transfer Out and Transfer In should match.
- Use this process only for warehouse-to-warehouse movement.


16. Stock Count

Purpose:
Stock count adjusts system stock based on physical counting.

Operations:
- Open Inventory > Stock Count.
- Create a stock count for a warehouse.
- Enter counted product quantities.
- Save the stock count.
- Review differences between system stock and physical stock.

Training Notes:
- Stock count should be done by authorized users only.
- Keep physical count records for audit.


17. Positive Adjustment

Purpose:
Positive adjustment increases stock for approved reasons outside normal receiving.

Operations:
- Open Inventory > Positive Adjustment.
- Select warehouse and product.
- Enter adjustment quantity and reason.
- Save the adjustment.
- Review transaction report after saving.

Training Notes:
- Use only for approved stock increase corrections.


18. Negative Adjustment

Purpose:
Negative adjustment decreases stock for approved reasons outside normal delivery or return.

Operations:
- Open Inventory > Negative Adjustment.
- Select warehouse and product.
- Enter adjustment quantity and reason.
- Save the adjustment.
- Review transaction report after saving.

Training Notes:
- Use only for approved stock decrease corrections.


19. Scrapping

Purpose:
Scrapping records damaged, expired, or unusable stock removed from inventory.

Operations:
- Open Inventory > Scrapping.
- Select warehouse and product.
- Enter scrapped quantity and reason.
- Save the scrapping record.
- Keep supporting approval or damage record if required.


20. Reports

Purpose:
Reports help users review transactions, stock position, sales, purchases, and inventory movement.

Modules:
- Sales Report
- Purchase Report
- Transaction Report
- Stock Report
- Movement Report

Operations:
- Open the required report page.
- Select filters such as date, product, customer, vendor, or warehouse if available.
- Review report results.
- Export or print where supported.
- Use reports for reconciliation and management review.


21. User Management

Purpose:
User management controls who can access the system.

Modules:
- Users
- Roles

Operations:
- Open Membership > Users.
- Create or update user accounts.
- Assign correct roles.
- Open Membership > Roles to review available role permissions.
- Disable or remove access for users who no longer need the system.

Training Notes:
- Do not give admin access to normal users.
- Review user access regularly.


22. Todo and Todo Item

Purpose:
Todo modules help track simple internal tasks.

Operations:
- Open Utilities > Todo.
- Create a todo list or task group.
- Open Utilities > Todo Item.
- Add task items under the relevant todo.
- Update task status as work progresses.


23. File and Image Handling

Purpose:
The application supports document and image storage for records where available.

Operations:
- Upload only valid files within the allowed file size.
- Use clear file names.
- Avoid uploading unrelated files.
- Check uploaded images/documents after saving.


24. Daily Operation Flow

Recommended daily flow:
- Login.
- Check dashboard.
- Review pending sales and purchase work.
- Enter goods receive, delivery, return, transfer, or adjustment records as needed.
- Review stock and transaction reports.
- Update todos or follow-up tasks.
- Logout.


25. Month-End Operation Flow

Recommended month-end flow:
- Complete all pending goods receive and delivery records.
- Complete purchase and sales returns.
- Review stock transfer status.
- Perform stock count where required.
- Review stock report and transaction report.
- Reconcile sales and purchase reports.
- Lock or archive records according to company policy if applicable.


26. Common Rules for Users

- Always select the correct customer, vendor, product, and warehouse.
- Do not create duplicate records.
- Do not delete records unless approved.
- Use adjustments only for approved corrections.
- Keep transaction dates accurate.
- Review reports regularly.
- Logout after completing work.


27. Suggested Training Plan

Day 1:
- Login, dashboard, navigation, profile, company setup.
- Master data: customers, vendors, products, warehouses.

Day 2:
- Sales order, delivery order, sales return.
- Purchase order, goods receive, purchase return.

Day 3:
- Transfer out, transfer in, stock count, adjustments, scrapping.

Day 4:
- Reports, user management, roles, daily and month-end operation practice.

Day 5:
- Practical test with sample transactions.
- Review common mistakes and approval rules.
